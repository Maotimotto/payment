## Design Context

### Product Name
汐账。The name frames scattered financial records as tides returning to a private ledger.

### Users
This is a local-first personal billing and bookkeeping tool for people who have transactions scattered across WeChat, Alipay, bank cards, CSV/PDF exports, and occasional screenshots. They use it to gather private financial records, resolve duplicates, classify spending, and inspect monthly cash flow without sending data to a server.

### Brand Personality
Private, composed, exacting. The interface should feel like a quiet personal ledger room: calm enough for sensitive money data, refined enough to be worth returning to, and precise enough to support review and cleanup.

### Aesthetic Direction
Lead with life horizon, not bookkeeping. The first screen is an immersive, full-viewport moving horizon: cinematic aerial video, deep tide ink, ivory paper, brass accents, restrained serif display type, tabular financial numerals, thin rules, and slow reveal motion. Bills are functional feedback below the fold, not the theme.

### Design Principles
- Keep the first screen emotional and cinematic: no permanent sidebar, no framed video card, and no dashboard-first composition.
- Let tools unfold after scroll: import, transaction cleanup, trends, and settings should be reachable, but secondary to perspective.
- Preserve trust: local-only privacy copy should be clear, quiet, and never alarmist.
- Treat bills as feedback, not fixation: the product helps the user understand their state, work return, and growth signals, then step away.
- Keep the life-view hero dominant: the product's emotional center is state, growth, and perspective; billing tools must remain quiet and usable.
- Cut dashboard noise: prioritize P0 charts and only surface extra insight when it changes a decision.
- Make uncertainty reviewable: duplicates, refunds, and uncategorized items should feel like a guided review queue.
- Use cinematic restraint: subtle transitions, material depth, and typography should carry the mood without slowing the app down.
